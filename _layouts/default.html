Here is a list of the thirty most influential investing and finance books that we've come across.

Check out our favourite investing books

From practical advice to the history of the worst financial crashes!

One Up On Wall Street

by Peter Lynch

"More than one million copies have been sold of this seminal book on investing in which legendary mutual-fund manager Peter Lynch explains the advantages that average investors have over professionals and how they can use these advantages to achieve financial success." (Simon and Schuster)
Wikipedia

The Little Book of Common Sense Investing

by Jack Bogle

"Whether you know it or not, Wall Street wants to steal your future. If you want to stop them, drop everything, read this marvelous little book, and take it to heart; your children, and their children's children, will thank you." (William Bernstein)
Wikipedia

The Intelligent Investor

by Benjamin Graham

“By far the best book on investing ever written.” (Warren Buffett)
Wikipedia

Where Are the Customers' Yachts

by Fred Schwed

"Once I picked it up I did not put it down. What Schwed has done is capture fully, in deceptively clean language, the lunacy at the heart of the investment business." (Michael Lewis)
Wikipedia

Beating the Street

by Peter Lynch

"Legendary money manager Peter Lynch explains his own strategies for investing and offers advice for how to pick stocks and mutual funds to assemble a successful investment portfolio." (Simon and Schuster)
Wikipedia

Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits and Other Writings

by Philip A. Fisher

"I sought out Phil Fisher after reading his Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits. When I met him, I was impressed by the man as by his ideas. A thorough understanding of the business, obtained by using Phil's techniques enables one to make intelligent investment commitments." (Warren Buffett)
Wikipedia

Stocks for the Long Run

by Jeremy Siegel

“A clearly written, neatly organized, highly persuasive exposition that lifts the veil of mystery from investing.” (Jack Bogle)
Wikipedia

Irrational Exuberance

by Robert J. Shiller

"A rare example of economic analysis, deeply respected within the discipline, wholly accessible to general readers." (Kirkus)
Wikipedia

The Big Short

by Michael Lewis

Lewis’s achievement with The Big Short is that he’s written a book that a huge number of people will love to read: it’s not just for finance geeks. (Felix Salomon)

Wikipedia

Too Big to Fail

by Andrew Ross Sorkin

"Andrew Ross Sorkin has written a fascinating, scene-by-scene saga of the eyeless trying to march the clueless through Great Recession." (Tom Wolfe)
Wikipedia

Liar's Poker

by Michael Lewis

“So memorable and alive. One of those rare works that encapsulate and define an era.” (Fortune)
Wikipedia

When Genius Failed

by Roger Lowenstein

“A riveting account that reaches beyond the market landscape to say something universal about risk and triumph, about hubris and failure.” (The New York Times)
Wikipedia

A Random Walk Down Wall Street

by Burton G. Malkiel

“Not more than half a dozen really good books about investing have been written in the past fifty years. This one may well belong in the classics category.” (Forbes)
Wikipedia

The Little Book of Behavioral Investing

by James Montier

"'Know thyself.' Overcoming human instinct is key to becoming a better investor. You would be irrational if you did not read this book." (Edward Bonham-Carter)
Wikipedia

Simple But Not Easy

by Richard Oldfield

"An expert, extremely readable real-life tour through the main features of the investment landscape" (Investors Chronicle)
Wikipedia

The Manual of Ideas

by John Mihaljevic

"Smart investors will read this book carefully and hope that few others do." (Bryan Lawrence)
Wikipedia

The Most Important Thing Illuminated

by Howard Marks

"If you take an exceptional talent and have them obsess about value investing for several decades, you may come up with a book as useful to value investors as this one." (Jeremy Grantham)
Wikipedia

The Great Crash 1929

by John Kenneth Galbraith

"Most intriguing for its depiction of the delusion that swept the culture, and the ways financiers and bankers, wishful academics and supine regulators willfully ignored reality and in the process encouraged the epic collapse of the stock market." (New York Times )
Wikipedia

Fooled by Randomness

by Nassim Taleb

"Taleb is Wall Street’s principal dissident. Fooled By Randomness is to conventional Wall Street wisdom approximately what Martin Luther’s ninety-nine theses were to the Catholic Church.” (Malcolm Gladwell)
Wikipedia

A Short History of Financial Euphoria

by John Kenneth Galbraith

"Galbraith traces the investor "herd" mentality from Tulipomania, which gripped Holland in the 1630s, through a variety of events and up through the 1987 stock market debacle--which he accurately predicted. He points out that the "mass escape from sanity by people in pursuit of profit," which, in his opinion, is always the cause, is never blamed." (Christopher Pavek)
Wikipedia

The Black Swan

by Nassim Taleb

“The Black Swan changed my view of how the world works.” (Daniel Kahneman)
Wikipedia

The Drunkard's Walk

by Leonard Mlodinow

“A wonderfully readable guide to how the mathematical laws of randomness affect our lives.” (Stephen Hawking)

Wikipedia

Against the Gods

by Peter L. Bernstein

"With his wonderful knowledge of the history and current manifestations of risk, Peter Bernstein brings us Against the Gods. Nothing like it will come out of the financial world this year or ever." (John Kenneth Galbraith)
Wikipedia

Why Stocks Go Up and Down

by William Pike

“An in-depth introduction to stock and bond investments. Assumes the reader has no prior background.” (GoodReads)
Wikipedia

Manias, Panics, and Crashes

by Charles P. Kindleberger

"Underneath the hilarious anecdotes, the elegant epigrams, and the graceful turns of phrase, Kindleberger is deadly serious. The manner in which humans beings earn their livings is no laughing matter to him, especially when they attempt to do so at the expense of one another." (Peter Bernstein)
Wikipedia

Extraordinary Popular Delusions and The Madness of Crowds

by Charles MacKay

"As with any true classic, once it is read it is hard to imagine not having known of it--and there is the compulsion to recommend it to others." (Andrew Tobias)
Wikipedia

Reminiscences of a Stock Operator

by Edwin Lefèvre

"An engaging read, chock-full of pearls of wisdom and amusing anecdotes, candid and analytical style evoking sympathy for the narrator." (Money Week)
Wikipedia

Clash of the Financial Pundits

by Joshua M. Brown

"Joshua Brown may be the funniest writer on finance today, but backstage Wall Street could make you cry more than laugh. The buffoons, manipulators, and incompetents Brown parades before us are the stewards of our retirement accounts." (Barron's)
Wikipedia

The Davis Dynasty

by John Rothchild

"This is an unusual biography, a rare gem that captures the history of one of Wall Street’s greatest families." (Barton Biggs)
Wikipedia
